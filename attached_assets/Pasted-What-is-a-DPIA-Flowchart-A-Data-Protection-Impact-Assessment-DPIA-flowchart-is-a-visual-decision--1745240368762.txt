What is a DPIA Flowchart?
A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) flowchart is a visual decision-making guide used to determine:

When a DPIA is required, based on the nature of data processing.

How to carry out the assessment, including key steps.

What actions to take based on the DPIA results.

It helps ensure organizations comply with privacy laws (like the GDPR) when processing personal data, especially when there's a high risk to individual rights or freedoms.

Here's a sample summary structure for a DPIA report:

1. Introduction
Organization name: ACME Corp

Assessment date: April 21, 2025

Purpose: Assess risks associated with implementing a new customer analytics system.

2. Description of the Processing
Type of data: Names, emails, behavior tracking, location

Data subjects: Customers using mobile app

Purpose: Personalized marketing and customer segmentation

3. Necessity & Proportionality
Legal basis: Consent and legitimate interest

Data minimization: Only relevant behavior metrics collected

Retention: 12 months

4. Risk Assessment

Risk Type	Likelihood	Impact	Risk Level
Unauthorized access	Medium	High	High
Data misuse by 3rd party	Low	High	Medium
Inadequate transparency	Medium	Medium	Medium
5. Mitigation Measures
Use end-to-end encryption

Two-factor authentication for admin access

Update privacy policy with transparent use cases

Limit data sharing to vetted vendors only

6. Consultation
Data Protection Officer (DPO) consulted

No consultation with supervisory authority needed

7. Final Decision
DPIA completed successfully.

Risks mitigated to acceptable level.

Approved by DPO and management.
