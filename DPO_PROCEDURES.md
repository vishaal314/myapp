## 3. Data Subject Rights Request Procedures

### 3.1 Request Reception and Acknowledgment

#### **Initial Response (Within 72 Hours)**
1. **Acknowledge Receipt**: Send confirmation email to requestor
2. **Identity Verification**: Request additional identification if needed
3. **Request Classification**: Categorize type of rights request
4. **Timeline Communication**: Inform requestor of response timeline (30 days)

#### **Request Categories**
- **Access Request (Article 15)**: Provide copy of personal data
- **Rectification (Article 16)**: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Erasure (Article 17)**: Delete personal data ("right to be forgotten")
- **Restriction (Article 18)**: Limit processing activities
- **Portability (Article 20)**: Provide data in structured format
- **Objection (Article 21)**: Stop specific processing activities

### 3.2 Request Processing Workflow

#### **Day 1-3: Initial Assessment**
- **Identity Verification**: Confirm requestor identity
- **Scope Determination**: Identify all relevant personal data
- **Legal Review**: Assess legal basis and any exemptions
- **Resource Allocation**: Assign responsible team members

#### **Day 4-25: Data Collection and Analysis**
- **System Search**: Query all databases and systems for personal data
- **Third-Party Coordination**: Contact service providers if necessary
- **Data Compilation**: Prepare comprehensive data package
- **Legal Review**: Ensure compliance with legal requirements

#### **Day 26-30: Response Preparation and Delivery**
- **Response Drafting**: Prepare detailed response letter
- **Data Package**: Compile personal data in readable format
- **Quality Review**: DPO final review before sending
- **Secure Delivery**: Send response via secure method

### 3.3 Special Considerations

#### **Complex Requests**
- **Extension Option**: Additional 60 days for complex requests
- **Fee Assessment**: Evaluate if fees apply for excessive requests
- **Third-Party Impact**: Consider impact on other individuals' rights
- **Legal Consultation**: Seek legal advice for unclear situations

#### **Rejection Scenarios**
- **Unfounded Requests**: Manifestly unfounded or excessive
- **Legal Conflicts**: Conflicts with legal obligations
- **Third-Party Rights**: Impact on rights and freedoms of others
- **Business Secrets**: Protection of trade secrets and intellectual property

## 4. Data Breach Response Procedures

### 4.1 Incident Detection and Initial Response

#### **Hour 0-2: Immediate Response**
1. **Incident Confirmation**: Verify breach occurrence and scope
2. **Containment Actions**: Immediate steps to stop the breach
3. **Team Activation**: Alert DPO and incident response team
4. **Preliminary Assessment**: Initial risk assessment

#### **Hour 2-6: Detailed Assessment**
1. **Scope Analysis**: Identify affected personal data and individuals
2. **Risk Evaluation**: Assess likely consequences for data subjects
3. **Root Cause**: Determine how the breach occurred
4. **Evidence Preservation**: Secure all relevant evidence

### 4.2 Regulatory Notification (72-Hour Rule)

#### **Dutch DPA Notification Requirements**
- **Timeline**: Within 72 hours of becoming aware
- **Content Required**:
  - Nature of the breach and affected data categories
  - Approximate number of affected data subjects
  - Contact details of DPO
  - Likely consequences of the breach
  - Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

#### **Notification Process**
1. **Draft Notification**: Prepare comprehensive breach report
2. **Legal Review**: Ensure compliance with notification requirements
3. **DPO Approval**: Final review and approval by DPO
4. **Submit to AP**: Official submission to Dutch Data Protection Authority
5. **Confirmation**: Obtain acknowledgment of receipt

### 4.3 Data Subject Notification

#### **High Risk Threshold Assessment**
Notify data subjects when breach is **likely to result in high risk** to rights and freedoms:
- **Identity theft risk**: Personal identification data compromised
- **Financial loss risk**: Payment or financial data involved
- **Reputational damage**: Sensitive personal information exposed
- **Physical harm**: Location or health data compromised

#### **Notification Content**
- **Clear language**: Plain language explanation of the breach
- **Data involved**: Types of personal data affected
- **Likely consequences**: Potential impact on individuals
- **Measures taken**: Steps taken to address the breach
- **Recommendations**: Actions individuals can take to protect themselves
- **Contact information**: How to contact DPO for questions

### 4.4 Documentation and Follow-up

#### **Record Keeping Requirements**
- **Breach Register**: Maintain comprehensive record of all breaches
- **Documentation**: Full details of facts, effects, and remedial action
- **Timeline**: Chronological record of all response activities
- **Lessons Learned**: Post-incident analysis and improvements

## 5. Privacy Impact Assessment (DPIA) Procedures

### 5.1 DPIA Trigger Assessment

#### **Mandatory DPIA Scenarios (Article 35)**
- **High risk processing**: Likely to result in high risk to individuals
- **Systematic monitoring**: Large-scale monitoring of public areas
- **Special categories**: Processing sensitive personal data at scale
- **Automated decision-making**: Significant effects on individuals

#### **DataGuardian Pro Specific Triggers**
- **New scanning algorithms**: Changes to PII detection methods
- **Data source expansion**: Adding new types of content scanning
- **International deployment**: Processing in new jurisdictions
- **AI integration**: Machine learning or automated analysis features

### 5.2 DPIA Process Workflow

#### **Phase 1: Preparation (Week 1-2)**
1. **Project Description**: Detailed description of processing activities
2. **Stakeholder Identification**: Key personnel and external parties
3. **Data Mapping**: Comprehensive data flow analysis
4. **Legal Basis Review**: Assessment of lawful basis for processing

#### **Phase 2: Risk Assessment (Week 3-4)**
1. **Risk Identification**: Systematic identification of privacy risks
2. **Impact Analysis**: Assessment of potential harm to data subjects
3. **Likelihood Assessment**: Probability of identified risks occurring
4. **Risk Rating**: Classification of risks (high/medium/low)

#### **Phase 3: Mitigation (Week 5-6)**
1. **Control Identification**: Technical and organizational measures
2. **Risk Reduction**: Evaluate effectiveness of proposed controls
3. **Residual Risk**: Assessment of remaining risks after mitigation
4. **Decision Making**: Go/no-go decision for processing activity

#### **Phase 4: Review and Approval (Week 7-8)**
1. **DPO Review**: Comprehensive assessment by DPO
2. **Stakeholder Consultation**: Input from affected parties
3. **Management Approval**: Senior management sign-off
4. **Documentation**: Final DPIA report and recommendations

### 5.3 DPIA Documentation Requirements

#### **Systematic Description**
- **Processing purposes**: Clear statement of why processing is necessary
- **Data categories**: Types of personal data processed
- **Data subjects**: Categories of individuals affected
- **Recipients**: Who will have access to the data
- **Third-country transfers**: Any transfers outside EU/EEA
- **Retention periods**: How long data will be kept

#### **Risk Assessment Matrix**
- **Likelihood**: Very Low / Low / Medium / High / Very High
- **Impact**: Negligible / Limited / Significant / Severe / Catastrophic
- **Risk Level**: Matrix combination determining overall risk
- **Mitigation**: Specific measures to reduce identified risks
