A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is a process required under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) to identify and minimize data protection risks in data processing activities‚Äîparticularly those likely to result in high risk to individuals‚Äô rights and freedoms.

üîó DPIA & GDPR: Legal Relevance
Under Article 35 of the GDPR, a DPIA is mandatory when data processing:

Involves systematic and extensive profiling

Involves large-scale processing of special categories (e.g., health data)

Includes monitoring of publicly accessible areas

Failing to conduct a required DPIA can lead to administrative fines of up to ‚Ç¨10 million or 2% of annual global turnover.

üìä DPIA Workflow (Step-by-Step)
Step 1: Identify the Need for a DPIA
Is the data processing new or significantly changed?

Could it impact the rights of individuals?

Use a screening checklist or flowchart.

Step 2: Describe the Processing
What is being done with the data?

Why is it being done?

Who is involved (controllers, processors)?

What data is being used?

Step 3: Assess Necessity & Proportionality
Is the processing necessary for your purpose?

Are you minimizing data collected?

Are the methods justified, legal, and fair?

Step 4: Identify and Assess Risks
Risks to data confidentiality, integrity, and availability

Risks to individual rights (e.g., profiling, discrimination)

Use a risk matrix to rate likelihood and severity

Step 5: Identify Mitigation Measures
Technical: encryption, access control, anonymization

Organizational: training, policies, audits

Legal: updating privacy notices, managing consents

Step 6: Sign-Off & Outcomes
Document the decision (proceed, revise, consult authority)

If high risk remains ‚Üí consult supervisory authority (e.g., ICO)

Retain DPIA as a living document ‚Äì revisit regularly

Step 7: Integration into Project Plan
Align with project timelines

Assign responsible parties

Include DPIA findings in design documentation
